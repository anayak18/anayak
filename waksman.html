Waksman Student Scholars Program: 2020 - present

Apurva Nayak July 24, 2021

I am a member of the Waksman Student Scholars Program that runs at WWP High School South. The club works in collaberation with the Waksman Institute of Microbiology at Rutgers University to conduct microbiology research. The objective of the club is to sequence the entire Landoltia punctata genome. The students conduct labratory procedures to select, transform, replicate and isolate a strand of DNA that is then processed by the Waksman institute. The waveforms of the DNA are then sent back to be analyzed by the students. The link to my clone from the 2020 school year can be found here.

Through this program, I have been able to publish research to the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI).

What I Learned

More than anything, Waksman piqued my curiosity into the world of research. The idea that as a highschooler I could be making contributions to national databases was shocking and sparked an interest that motivated me to study for the entrance exam, and after I got in to analyze my clone as best I could. I look forward to continue researching, both in and out of the club, and I am ever-grateful to this club for giving me such an amazing experience.
